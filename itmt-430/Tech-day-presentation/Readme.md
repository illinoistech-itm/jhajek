# Final Presentation Guidelines

Congratulations, you have made it through your 4 years and are about to head on to your next great journey in life. This is your chance to show off to the world all the great work you have done and the things you have learned in your time at Illinois Tech. This is the outline of what is required for the April 27th Monday presentation. 

## Professor Will Provide

In an opening presentation along with this material typed up and distributed to guests, so you know what will be provided and you don’t need to repeat.

* Background of the Cloud Platform (with Images) and hardware description
* Short conceptual rundown of Cloud Native applications
* Short run down of the network given to you
* Short rundown of the tools used
 * Packer
 * Terraform
 * Vault
 * Consul
 * Proxmox
 * API keys
* Short display of the example/starter code given to you

## What students need to provide

You will have 5-10 minutes to present (best to have two presenters – someone on the computer and someone talking)

* The amount of time depends on the number of teams presenting
* In order to present you must have at least Sprint-04 deployed and a working usable site

### Tell us a story of the complete journey

Required:

* Demonstrate the functionality of your site (login and take a few actions)
* Discuss your tech stack (from the Application point of view)
* Show (quickly) your GitHub commit frequency page
* Show your project management screen
* Number of VMs you deployed
* Discuss your toughest bug that you solved, and the coolest feature your team designed
* Discuss one thing your group learned or that you want others to know


### Is this a deliverable?

Note this is a separate presentation and will the take place of the normative PM presentation but the regular report.md and critiques are still due as always. No deliverable will be turned in for this presentation Monday April 27th, but you should organize your presentation ahead of time in PowerPoint or a Readme.md file

Event will be live-streamed by OTS on YouTube (link in the PDF invite document) you will have access to the entire recording for reposting/editing your part – shortly after the presentation day.

### Dress Code

For presenting teams it will be business casual. For non-presenting students please show up to support your fellow presenters.
